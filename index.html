About

I'm a software engineer and Washington State University Data Analytics graduate (3.62 GPA, minors in Mathematics and Criminal Justice). By day I'm a Quality Engineer at HP, where I build internal web tools and test-automation programs, rebuild data infrastructure, and turn ambiguous business and regulatory requirements into reliable tooling and reporting.

Outside of work I ship complete products end to end. For real clients I've built and deployed production websites — like The Pooch Pit, a dog-grooming booking platform with Stripe deposits, a double-booking-proof availability engine, transactional email, and a no-code admin dashboard the owner runs herself — plus a fine-art e-commerce storefront, both live on custom domains with Cloudflare and Railway-managed Postgres.

I also push my engineering with passion projects: real-time multiplayer strategy games with authoritative servers and bot AI, a basketball roguelike published on the App Store, and a full-stack cigar-collection PWA with native mobile builds. They're for fun, but they're where I take on the hardest systems problems.

I work across the stack in TypeScript, React, Node, and Next.js, with a data and ML background in Python and R. I like hard problems and shipping things people actually rely on.
